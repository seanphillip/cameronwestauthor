Memoir · Psychology

First Person Plural

Blackstone Publishing · May 26, 2026 · Originally published 1999

Described as "a page-turning journey through hell" by Entertainment Weekly, this 25th Anniversary Edition is a riveting, spellbinding memoir that boldly illuminates the truth behind Dissociative Identity Disorder and reveals the deep humanity behind the condition. Featuring a new Epilogue with the first physiological evidence of alter personalities via EEG brain wave patterns, and an overview of current research on a condition now known to affect an estimated 100 million people worldwide.

A New York Times, Publishers Weekly, and USA Today bestseller reaching #1 on Amazon and Barnes & Noble, sold in 22 countries. Disney Studios purchased the movie rights. Cameron and Rikki appeared on Oprah twice.

Fiction · Mystery & Thriller

The Medici Dagger

Atria Books · January 17, 2002

"Intellectually provocative… surprising and chilling." — Pittsburgh Post-Gazette

1491: Leonardo da Vinci looks upon his most dangerous invention and knows he must hide it from the world. Five centuries later, the race to find it begins. When a plane goes down over the Atlantic carrying a page from da Vinci's journals — and a museum curator's home burns to the ground — the tragedies seem unconnected. Twenty years later, Hollywood stuntman Reb Barnett refuses to believe they were accidents. He's sent to Italy on a desperate quest to find da Vinci's Circles of Truth, a coded 15th-century map pointing to the Medici Dagger — a weapon so remarkable it's worth a fortune to modern arms dealers, and worth even more to Reb as his only link to the truth about his father's death.
